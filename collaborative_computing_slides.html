The Core Question

These systems were deliberately designed. Someone made choices about what they allow, what they prevent, and who benefits. That's what this field studies — understanding those choices and learning how to make better ones.

Core Concepts
Two Fields, One Vision
Social Computing
  • User-generated content — Wikipedia, Reddit, YouTube. The platform doesn't create value. The users do.
  • Collective intelligence — Millions of independent contributions produce knowledge no single expert could.
  • Community formation — People self-organize around shared interests, identities, and goals.
  • Social feedback loops — Upvotes, retweets, likes shape what gets seen and what gets buried.
CSCW — Groupware
  • Communication — Exchange of messages and information. Email, chat, video calls, shared docs.
  • Coordination — Managing dependencies between tasks. Who does what, in what order, by when.
  • Cooperation — Joint production of a shared artifact — a document, codebase, or design system.
  • Awareness — Knowing what your collaborators are doing and where they are in their work.
Traditional computing = one person + one machine.   Collaborative computing = many people interacting with each other through machines. The computer becomes a medium for social activity — not just a tool.
Theoretical Foundation  ·  Johansen, 1988

Real-World Impact
Platforms That Changed How We Work
Wikipedia
55M Articles · 300+ Languages
  • Nature study: accuracy rivals Encyclopaedia Britannica for science articles
  • Governance: Talk Pages, full Revision History, reputation-based moderation
  • Automated bots patrol 24/7 — reverting vandalism, flagging missing citations
  • Key lesson: Quality crowdsourcing needs governance, not just technology
GitHub
200M+ Repos · 100M Developers
  • Strangers shipping software that runs the internet — fully distributed
  • Pull Requests = peer review built directly into the workflow
  • Improves quality AND transfers knowledge between teammates simultaneously
  • Asynchronous CSCW at its most powerful and mature form
Google Docs
1 Billion+ Daily Users
  • Operational Transformation — two people type simultaneously without breaking each other's work
  • Live cursors = workspace awareness made visible and tangible
  • The complexity is invisible — that invisibility is the achievement
Slack / Teams
Replaced Email for 65% of Enterprises
  • Channels = persistent, searchable, threaded group communication
  • Research shows IM used for substantive work — not just casual chat
  • Workers switch to IM even when richer channels like video are available
Critical Challenges
These Systems Are
Not Neutral

Grudin warned us in 1994 about eight problems with collaborative systems. Many still persist today — some are worse than ever.

Privacy & Surveillance
Workflow systems log who did what, when. In Europe, monitoring is legally restricted. When workers discover they are watched, trust collapses — and people optimize for the metric, not the actual goal.
Misinformation
False stories travel faster than corrections — they are more emotionally engaging. Algorithms optimized for engagement amplify divisive content. Coordinated fake accounts manufacture false consensus at massive scale.
The 90-9-1 Rule
90% of users lurk. 9% contribute occasionally. 1% create almost everything. That tiny 1% shapes what the entire community looks like — their biases become the community's biases, for everyone.
The Digital Divide
Reliable internet, modern devices, digital literacy — prerequisites not equally available globally. The benefits of collaborative computing are real, but they are not evenly shared across the world.
Brooks' Law: Adding more people to a late software project makes it later — coordination costs multiply faster than productivity gains.
2025 & Beyond
The Next Wave of Collaboration
🤖
AI Agents as Active Collaborators
Microsoft 365 Copilot, GitHub Copilot, Slack bots — AI now drafts documents, summarises meetings, routes tasks without being asked. Key question: when AI contributes to a team's work, who is accountable?
🥽
Spatial & Mixed Reality Collaboration
Apple Vision Pro, Meta Quest 3 — virtual meeting rooms where remote colleagues appear life-sized. Collaborative 3D environments. Spatial computing may recover what remote work has always lacked: physical presence awareness.
🔗
Federated & Decentralised Social Networks
Mastodon (ActivityPub), Bluesky (AT Protocol) — social platforms with no single controlling company. Users own their data. Communities set their own rules. The most important governance experiment in internet history.
🧠
Human-AI Collective Intelligence
AI-moderated deliberation platforms. Intelligent crowdsourcing matching tasks to best-qualified humans. Hybrid prediction markets. Goal: combine human creativity and judgment with AI's scale and pattern recognition.
